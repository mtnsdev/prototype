15 — Defensibility

Three data moats,
compounding daily

Enable’s competitive advantage is not feature depth — it is data that no competitor can replicate. Three proprietary intelligence assets compound with every advisor interaction.

Conversational intelligence corpus

Every advisor team call, supplier meeting, and strategy session captured through Fireflies integration. This is not generic training data — it is months of real advisor conversations revealing actual booking patterns, client objection handling, supplier negotiation tactics, and product knowledge transfer.

Zero cold start. The corpus exists before Enable launches. Every week of operation adds structured intelligence that no new entrant can approximate. Competitors would need years of embedded access to replicate what Enable inherits on day one.

DMC & supplier intelligence

The most trusted knowledge in luxury travel is the least documented: which DMC handles high-net-worth ground logistics in Patagonia, which concierge at The Brando actually delivers, which operators have capacity during peak. This intelligence lives in advisor memory and private networks.

Enable captures this through structured ingestion from advisor workflows — email forwards, trip debriefs, supplier evaluations. Each data point is tagged, attributed, and searchable. A network effect emerges: every advisor who contributes raises the intelligence floor for the entire platform.

The Enable Data Layer

As the platform aggregates product intelligence, supplier evaluations, commission structures, and booking patterns across hundreds of agencies, Enable builds a proprietary luxury travel data layer — structured, anonymised industry intelligence that no individual agency could assemble alone.

This data layer becomes a strategic asset beyond the SaaS subscription: packaged industry benchmarks, destination intelligence, supplier performance indices, and trend analysis that Enable can license back to the ecosystem — consortia, tourism boards, hotel groups, and DMC networks. The platform that powers advisor workflows also becomes the definitive intelligence source for the luxury travel industry.

The defensibility equation

Feature parity is achievable. Data parity is not. Enable’s moat widens with every conversation captured, every supplier evaluated, every client preference recorded. The cost of replication increases daily while the value to each advisor increases with network scale.

16 — Privacy by Design

Client data as
sacred trust

Luxury travel clients expect absolute discretion. Enable’s architecture treats privacy not as a compliance checkbox but as a core product principle.

01

Advisor-scoped by default

VIC profiles are visible only to the advisor who owns the relationship. No cross-advisor data leakage. Client intelligence stays within the trust boundary of the existing relationship.

02

Full audit trails

Every data access, search query, and profile view is logged. Agency principals have complete visibility into who accessed what client data and when — essential for UHNW client management.

03

Governed sharing

When collaboration is required — handoffs, team bookings, specialist referrals — sharing is explicit, permissioned, and revocable. Advisors control exactly which information crosses relationship boundaries.
